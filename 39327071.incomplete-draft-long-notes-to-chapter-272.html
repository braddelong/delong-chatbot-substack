9) For a long time, Friedman argued that any policy that kept what he called the quantity of money—checking account deposits, plus cash, plus small and immediately accessible savings deposits—growing at a constant pace—would, if followed consistently and religiously, produce an inflation- and high unemployment-free economy. But as Charles Goodhart had warned him, the correlations he relied on between the money stock and spending broke down as soon as central banks started to try to rely on them as control mechanisms. See Charles Goodhart, “Problems of Monetary Management: The U.K. Experience”, Reserve Bank of Australia Papers in Monetary Economics 1 (1975) <https://link.springer.com/book/10.1007/978-1-349-17295-5>.

But did Eisenhower represent a consensus, or was he himself a powerful actor who returned American conservatism to sanity—for a while—by forcing underground and into silence the leaders who wanted to destroy all the works of THAT COMMUNIST ROOSEVELT? Joe McCarthy had real power in the Republican Party in the first half of the 1950s—Trump’s hold on the party in the early 2020s is a reasonable analogy, for Republicans were as scared to cross McCarthy then as, say, Mike Pence is to cross Trump and condemn Trump for welcoming plans to lynch Pence on January 6, 2021. Republican Senate majority leader Robert Taft was quite a right-wing case in himself. The Republicans chose Eisenhower as their standard-bearer because they wanted to maximize their chances of winning. And Eisenhower devoted himself to curbing the crazy swamps. But even Eisenhower made the Democrats and the press do the heavy anti-McCarthy lifting. Plus Earl Warren in his old age turned out to be a very different kind of Republican than anyone before would ever have imagined he would or could be.

There is a counterfactual in which America’s politics of the 1950s looks a lot more like the Nixonian early 1970s—break the country in half and come away with the larger half—or the Trumpist late 2010s—break the country in half and come away with the smaller half.
